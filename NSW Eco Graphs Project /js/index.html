Introduction to Economics

Production Possibility Frontier (PPF)

The PPF shows the maximum combinations of two goods that can be produced with given resources. Movement along the curve shows opportunity cost (trade-offs). Shift of the curve shows economic growth or decline.

Drag the point on the curve to see the opportunity cost in action.

Circular Flow of Income

A model showing the flows of money and goods between sectors of the economy. Leakages (S, T, M) reduce economic activity, while injections (I, G, X) increase it.

Hover or click on sectors to see their role.

Basic Demand and Supply

Shifts of Curves

Price Elasticity of Demand

Measures responsiveness of quantity demanded to a price change. See how steepness relates to elasticity and total revenue.

Adjust elasticity to see the effect on the curve shape.

Price Elasticity of Supply

Measures responsiveness of quantity supplied to a price change. Steeper means more inelastic.

Adjust elasticity to see the effect on the curve shape.

Market Intervention (Price Ceiling / Floor)

Governments may intervene to set max (ceiling) or min (floor) prices, creating shortages or surpluses.

Free market equilibrium.

Markets

Multiple Simultaneous Shocks

When both Demand and Supply shift at the same time, the effect on either Price or Quantity will be ambiguous depending on the magnitude of the shifts.

Try shifting both curves to see the ambiguous effects.

Labour Markets

Labour Market (Demand and Supply of Labour)

Labour demand comes from firms (derived demand), labour supply from individuals. Wage is the price of labour.

Equilibrium wage and employment level.

Financial Markets

Loanable Funds Market

Supply of funds (savings) and demand for funds (borrowing/investment). Determines interest rates.

Equilibrium interest rate.

Money Market (RBA Monetary Policy)

Money supply is perfectly inelastic (vertical) as it's controlled by the RBA via Domestic Market Operations. Demand is downward sloping (liquidity preference).

Move the slider to see how RBA operations affect the cash rate.

Foreign Exchange Market

Supply and demand of AUD against other currencies. Determines the Exchange Rate (e.g., USD/AUD).

Equilibrium floating exchange rate.

The Global Economy

Foreign Exchange (Extended - Fixed/Managed)

Shows intervention by the central bank in the forex market to maintain a fixed or managed exchange rate.

Floating exchange rate determined by market forces.

J-Curve

Illustrates how a currency depreciation initially worsens the trade balance (valuation effect) before improving it (volume effect) over time.

Click Animate to trace the effect over time.

Economic Issues

Aggregate Demand / Aggregate Supply (AD/AS)

The macroeconomic model determining overall Price Level (inflation) and Real GDP (economic growth/unemployment).

Economy at macroeconomic equilibrium.

Business Cycle

Fluctuations of economic activity (GDP) around a long-term growth trend.

Hover along the curve to see the phases (Expansion, Peak, Contraction, Trough).

Phillips Curve

The short-run trade-off between inflation and unemployment. Movement shows this trade-off. Shift shows structural changes (e.g., stagflation).

Drag slider for short-run trade-off.

Lorenz Curve

A visual representation of income or wealth inequality. Further from the line of perfect equality means higher inequality (higher Gini coefficient).

Gini Coefficient: ~0.30

Economic Policies

Monetary Policy Transmission Mechanism

How a change in the RBA cash rate flows through the economy to affect inflation and economic growth.

Select a policy change to begin.

Fiscal Policy on AD/AS

Government budget changes affect Aggregate Demand. An expansionary budget shifts AD right; contractionary shifts AD left.

Budget: Balanced. Economy at equilibrium.
